Learning Mandarin

Learning Mandarin is a hobby I picked up in sophomore year of college, after a long time of being interested in the language and culture. In 2016, I visited Xi'an on an educational exchange trip, and living there with a host family broadened my perspective on the world. Learning Mandarin is a way for me to challenge myself to embrace new ideas and experiences, and to develop relationships with Chinese speakers both within the United States and abroad.

Stats

I've been studying for 2+ years, averaging 2.5 class hours per week. I received my HSK 3 Certification in August 2024, with a score of 295/300.

Hiking

I enjoy hiking because being surrounded by nature is soothing. I love to explore along trails, paying attention to the birds, insects, and other animals I can see and hear. I'm not a competitive or destination-oriented hiker, and I am regularly passed by people decades older than me with hiking poles and determination. The most important thing for me is to be mindful and appreciate the beauty wherever I am.

Aerial Arts

Although I haven't been a practicing aerialist in several years, this was an important part of my life growing up. I worked as an instructor as a teenager and performed in several shows and competitions. Similar to gymnastics, aerial is a skill that builds discipline and strength at high levels. Aerial fabrics (silks) also require careful attention to detail, because performers rely on specific wraps and knots to keep ourselves safe.
